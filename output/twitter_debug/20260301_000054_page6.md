We build an mvp for this fun benchmark that challenges frontier LLMs like never before.

The task: giving a model a startup to run, and see if it goes bankrupt.

The result: GPT-5.2 (and sometimes Sonnet 4.6) cannot beat a sub-optimal greedy baseline, and quickly goes bankrupt.

\- Why we build it: This benchmark models after an increasingly important agentic scenario that very few models is optimized for: long-term coherence.

If you are a researcher, you would wish you can leave the agent at night to supervise a training run. If you are a manager, you would wish the agent can delegate tasks and check in from time to time.

But models right now are like customer-service agents. They answer your question, solve your task, and then dip. Therefore, we NEED a benchmark that evaluates long-term coherence to prepare models for future work. YC Bench (Your Company Bench) evaluates this in a very systematic way. We build an entire simulation, populated with work, money, and employees, to ask the model to be a startup founder for three years.

This job is hard in several ways. Employees have only a fixed bandwidth so the model cannot assign too many tasks. They also have specialization and different experience level so the model must balance them out. Also, client jobs have different perks and punishments so the model needs to choose wisely. On top of that, there is a monthly payroll that increases as the employee gets better.

All of these put insane pressure on most models, causing their startup to go out of business in a mere few months.

\- What we've learned: we find that most models cannot outperform a simple greedy baseline, that always choose the best task to accomplish right now given some criterion. The greedy baseline does not compute long-term consequences at all, so failing to catch up with it is surprising.

We also find that Gemini-3-Flash as a medium-size model outperforms both the frontiers (Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5.2) and matches the baseline, while switching to a long-term strategy.

We find interesting thought processes in a scratchpad we give to each model. For example, the winning Gemini Flash model is able to identify the specialized domains (eg. model training) that have the highest return given the employees at hand, and when it reaches 1M revenue, switch to optimize only for those tasks. This type of multi-stage, long-term strategy is an intriguing property of LLMs and something that is very valuable in real life.

In the near future, we want to make the benchmark even more realistic. For example, we want the model to be able to hire/fire employees, maintaining client meetings and negotiations, introducing LLM NPCs.

Let us know what you wanna see happening in YC Bench to your favorite SOTA LLM!
