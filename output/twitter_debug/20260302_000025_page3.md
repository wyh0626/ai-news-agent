Recommend another deep analysis of DeepSeek's new paper from Zhihu contributor AI解码师. ⚙️🔥

💡 The instinctive reaction to I/O bottlenecks?
Spend more money → Bigger NICs, faster storage, more nodes.
👉DualPath does the opposite — No new hardware. Instead, it unlocks idle capacity already sitting in the system.

This mirrors DeepSeek's earlier training-side work:
• DualPipe overlaps forward/backward communication to fill "bubble time"
• EPLB (Expert Parallel Load Balancer) smooths out MoE expert imbalance and eliminates wasted compute
So, a clear, consistent engineering philosophy emerges:
‼️Don't scale hardware first. Drain every last drop of unused capacity, which is the same mindset behind training V3 with just 2,048 H800s, and why they keep emphasizing "hardware-driven model design" in its co-design papers.

🤖 Why this paper focuses on Agentic inference
In standard chat scenarios:
• Short conversations and contexts of only a few thousand tokens
• KV cache is small → storage rarely becomes a bottleneck
But agent workloads are completely different:
• Dozens or even hundreds of tool calls
• Repeated reasoning and result aggregation
• KV cache keeps accumulating, pushing context to 128K tokens or more
• Requests arrive in bursts, each carrying massive historical context
This workload completely overwhelms the traditional single-path (Storage → Prefill) design.
DualPath is essentially forced into existence by the combination of long context, high concurrency and bursty request patterns. And as Agent applications scale, this problem will only intensify.

📊 Why the results matter
The paper evaluates a 660B production-scale model — almost certainly DeepSeek-V3 or a close variant.
Achieving nearly 2× throughput improvement at this scale is fundamentally different from gains on small models. The engineering value is real.

🧠 A broader signal
This work is co-authored with Peking University and Tsinghua University.
DeepSeek's infra research is now generating clear academic spillover, not just internal wins.
While the community is busy watching GitHub and waiting for "V4", DeepSeek quietly drops heavyweight systems papers on arXiv.

⚖️In the end, better inference efficiency & better model capability are equivalent to users:
Either make the model smarter or make the same intelligence dramatically cheaper.

First empirical study on how developers are actually writing AI context files across open-source projects.
